The Hybrid Optical Architecture

Bypassing the Von Neumann bottleneck and the thermal limits of traditional electrical interconnects through silicon photonics and hybrid FPGA integration.

1. The Thermal Wall & Bandwidth Crisis

In modern High-Performance Computing (HPC) and AI training clusters, computation is no longer the primary bottleneck—data movement is. Moving terabytes of data across electrical copper traces at high frequencies generates massive impedance and, consequently, extreme thermal output. Current systems spend more power cooling the interconnects than computing the data.

2. The HOCS Paradigm (Hybrid E/O Integration)

The Hybrid Optical Computing System (HOCS) acts as a physical-layer translation engine. Instead of forcing data through copper, HOCS captures the electrical signals outputted by standard processing cores (such as high-performance SoMs and FPGAs) and instantly modulates them into optical pulses.

E/O Conversion

Proprietary micro-ring resonators and electro-absorption modulators translate digital electrical bits into discrete wavelengths of light with sub-nanosecond latency.

Zero-Heat Waveguides

Once data is in the optical domain, it travels through silicon waveguides. Photons do not generate resistive heat, enabling dense packing of data channels without thermal throttling.

3. Theoretical Throughput Calculation

Our target bandwidth of 4.14 TB/s is achieved through Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM), allowing multiple independent data streams to share a single optical physical medium simultaneously.

T_total = N_{channels} × R_{baud} × M_{modulation}
Where:
- T_total: Aggregate system throughput (Target: 4.14 TB/s)
- N_{channels}: Number of independent optical wavelengths multiplexed
- R_{baud}: Symbol rate per channel
- M_{modulation}: Bits per symbol (e.g., PAM4 = 2 bits/symbol)

By scaling N_{channels} across the photonic fabric, HOCS linearly scales bandwidth while keeping thermal dissipation (P_thermal) exponentially lower than equivalent copper configurations (P_thermal ≈ I²R).

4. FPGA / Edge Computing Synergy

HOCS is designed to interface with programmable hardware. By utilizing high-speed transceivers on FPGA platforms, our system acts as an ultra-wide, zero-latency bridge between distributed compute nodes, making it ideal for scalable AI inference and large-scale data center topologies.
